Responsible play

The lottery is meant to be a bit of fun, never a way to make money or escape problems. This is an information-only site — you can't play or pay here — but wherever you play, these habits help you stay in control.

$

Set a budget

Decide an amount you can comfortably afford to lose before you play, and treat daily, weekly or monthly limits as firm.

⏸

Take a break

Step away regularly. If it stops being fun or starts feeling like a chase, take a cool-off — a day, a week, or longer.

🔒

Self-exclusion

If you need to stop, licensed operators let you self-exclude for six months or more. Don't hesitate to use it.

Worried about your play, or someone else's?

Confidential help is available 24/7. Add your local support service here (for example, a national gambling-helpline phone number, website, or live chat) so players can reach it in one click.
